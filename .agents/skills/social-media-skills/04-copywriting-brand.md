# Copywriting & Brand

## copywriting
**Path:** `/Users/charankumar/skills/copywriting/`
**Source:** community

Conversion-focused marketing copy for landing pages, emails, and social.

### Core Principles
- Clarity > cleverness
- Benefits > features
- Specificity > buzzwords
- Honesty > hype
- Customer language > company language

### Mandatory Process
1. **Context Gathering** — Page purpose, audience, product/offer, traffic source
2. **Copy Brief Lock** — Present brief summary for confirmation before writing
3. **Write** — Organized by section with headline/subheadline/CTA/body
4. **Alternatives** — 2-3 options for headlines and CTAs with rationales
5. **Annotations** — Why each choice was made

### Writing Style
- Simple > complex | Active > passive | Confident > hedged
- Show outcomes instead of adjectives
- No fabricated data, testimonials, or guarantees

### Structure Framework
- **Above the fold:** Headline (single most important message) + Subheadline + Primary CTA
- **Core sections:** Social proof -> Problem articulation -> Solution & benefits -> How it works -> Objection handling -> Final CTA with risk reduction

### Feature → Benefit → Outcome
Always connect features to what the user actually gains.

## brand-guidelines
**Path:** `/Users/charankumar/skills/brand-guidelines/`
**Source:** community (Sentry brand guidelines)

Write copy following brand guidelines — covers Plain Speech (default) and Voice tones.

### Tone Selection
| Use Plain Speech | Use Voice |
|------------------|-----------|
| Product UI, buttons, labels | 404 pages, empty states |
| Documentation, error messages | Onboarding, loading states |
| Settings, transactional emails | "What's New", marketing copy |

### Plain Speech Rules
1. Be concise — fewest words needed
2. Be direct — tell users what to do
3. Use active voice — "Save" not "Changes will be saved"
4. Avoid jargon
5. Be specific — "3 errors found" not "Some errors"

### Spelling & Grammar
- American English (color, not colour)
- Title Case for headings, Sentence case for body text/buttons
- No exclamation marks in UI, no periods in short labels

### Best for Social Media
The Voice tone works well for brand social media copy — empathetic, self-aware, occasionally witty or snarky in appropriate moments.

## brand-perception-psychologist
**Path:** `/Users/charankumar/skills/brand-perception-psychologist/`
**Source:** community

Brand Psychologist and Semiotics Researcher — diagnose what a brand's visual, verbal, and behavioral signals communicate subconsciously.

### Framework: Brand Schema Alignment

**Step 1** — Identify current brand schema (subconscious impression)
**Step 2** — Compare to intended position (desired perception)
**Step 3** — Find the largest mismatch (strongest signal conflict)
**Step 4** — Prescribe the smallest useful correction (highest-leverage change)
**Step 5** — Verify cross-touchpoint consistency

### Decision Matrix
| Gap Size | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Small | Targeted refinements |
| Medium | Realign strongest mismatched layer |
| Large | Rework identity system across all layers |

### Failure Modes
- Changing the logo and calling it repositioning (must align visual + verbal + behavioral)
- Mixed messages across touchpoints (creates dissonance)
- Ignoring category schema and forcing new meaning too quickly
